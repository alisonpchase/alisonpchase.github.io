I'm currently a Senior Research Scientist at the [Applied Physics Laboratory](https://www.apl.washington.edu/home.php) and Affiliate Assistant Professor in the School of Oceanography, at the University of Washington in Seattle, WA USA. I am an optical and biological oceanographer, motivated by understanding the dynamics of changing ocean ecosystems. My research is focused on methods development using imaging-in-flow cytometry, in situ optical proxies, and ocean color remote sensing data to combine knowledge from ship-board measurements and Earth-observing satellites. I subsequently apply these data and methods to unravel questions about how phytoplankton communities are distributed and how these distributions are changing on varying space and time scales. This information is critical to our understanding of the marine ecosystem, the health of the ocean, and the dynamics of fisheries. I collaborate with data science experts to actively incorporate machine learning and AI, cloud computing, and open-source data processing workflows into my research. 

I recieved my PhD in Oceanography from the University of Maine, co-advised by Emmanuel Boss and Lee Karp-Boss, and then spent three years as a
[Washington Research Foundation Postdoctoral Fellow](https://www.wrfseattle.org/grants/fellows/) at the University of Washington Applied Physics Laboratory.
